# Characters

## Species

The mortal species of the world are generally classified into three categories: men, mer, and beastfolk. {% bemph "Man" %} refers to any of the four human phenotypes, namely the Bretons, Imperials, Nords, and Redguards. While the Bretons are the result of interbreeding between Aldmeri and Nedes, they are most commonly considered men, not mer. {% bemph "Mer" %} or Elves compose the Altmer, Bosmer, Dunmer, and Orsimer. What sets the mer apart from the other species is their long lifespans. Aside from the Orsimer, Aldmeri lifespans typically range from several centuries to as much as a thousand years, if sufficiently supported by life-extending sorceries. The term {% bemph "Beastfolk" %}, also called Betmer, is used to refer to any sentient animal-like humanoid, such as the Argonians, Gnolls, Khajiit, and Sload. The term was popularised by the Reman Empire as propaganda to justify their invasion of Khajiiti lands by likening them to cats, and the term has stuck ever since. While there exists anecdotal evidence of otherwise unintelligent creatures, such as trolls and mudcrabs, being capable of speech or higher intelligence; these are usually unique exceptions or simply fiction.

They regard themselves as direct descendants of the original spirits and as the purest, most unaltered heirs of the Aldmer, the ancient elves who first arrived on Tamriel from the mythical lost continent of Aldmeris. High elves are renowned as one of the most intelligent species, and their exceptional innate aptitude for the arcane arts makes them the most gifted sorcerers. This affinity is further enhanced by their naturally long lifespans. Altmeri society has been shaped by centuries of scholarly refinement and is deeply hierarchical and tradition-bound. The Altmer have long pursued an ideal of cultural perfection, venerating their lineage and striving to preserve the traditions, knowledge, and practices of their ancient ancestors. High elves are often perceived by other species as proud, aloof, or even arrogant. The Altmer, for their part, consider themselves Tamriel's most civilised people. The common tongue is derived from Altmeri speech and writing, and much of the human empires’ art, law, architecture, and scholarship can trace its roots back to Altmeri tradition.

      {% bemph "Argonians" %}, or Saxhleel in their native language of Jel, are the reptilian natives of Black Marsh, a vast swampland province in southeastern Tamriel. Agile and cunning, Argonians possess a natural resistance to poison and disease, a trait that serves them well in their treacherous home. They are swift swimmers and can breathe underwater. They are also naturally adept spellcasters. Argonian society is characterised by two distinct cultural streams that result in a significant internal dichotomy across the province. The first branch of civilisation comprises the ziggurat builders, who construct massive step pyramids that serve as the cultural centres for their bustling cities. These settled communities engage in domestic and foreign trade, create well-crafted, durable goods, and pursue academic knowledge. Conversely, the second branch comprises those Argonians who view their kinsfolk's preoccupation with permanence as an ill-advised obsession with stasis. They lead a nomadic lifestyle within the deep marshes, living in temporary dwellings made from mud. They craft only what they need, and nothing is intended to last. The latter group is often perceived as savage or animalistic. However, despite these divisions, both factions see themselves as one people.

    <p>
      The {% bemph "Bosmer" %}, also called Wood Elves by men, are the nimble elves of Valenwood, a forested province in southwestern Tamriel. They are commonly slight of stature and sometimes sport antlers or horns. They deliberately reject the rigid formality of Aldmeri high culture in favor of a simpler existence in harmony with nature. Exceptionally agile and quick-witted, wood elves excel as scouts and thieves, and are universally recognised as the finest archers. Bosmeri society in Valenwood is deeply intertwined with the Green Pact, a strict religious edict mandated by Y'ffre, the Spirit of the Now, which absolutely forbids the harming of any vegetation within Valenwood. Consequently, traditional Bosmer are exclusively carnivorous and practise ritual cannibalism. They utilise bone and animal products for their constructions and rely entirely on foreign imports for plant-based materials. However, Bosmer living outside of Valenwood usually regard these strictures far less rigidly. During the Interregnum, the Bosmer were absorbed into the Aldmeri Dominion. The alliance has been rocky, though, given that many Wood Elves perceive the Dominion as &raquo;&hairsp;polluting&hairsp;&laquo; Valenwood.

    <p>
      {% bemph "Bretons" %}, from the Ehlnofex word &raquo;&hairsp;beratu&hairsp;&laquo; meaning &raquo;&hairsp;half&hairsp;&laquo;, are a hybrid of both human and elven ancestry that primarily inhabit High Rock in northwestern Tamriel. This mixed ancestry gives them a unique perspective on the eternal conflict between men and mer. Bretons possess an innate talent for the magical arts, often attributed to their Aldmeri blood, and are known for their intelligence and quick, perceptive grasp of spellcraft. However, their shorter lifespans place them at a disadvantage compared to their elven forbears. By and large, Bretons are pale-skinned and lightly built, less muscular than other men, with rounded, softer features. Some even display pointed ears much like elves. They are found all across the continent, often as merchants or spellsword mercenaries. The province of High Rock itself is fractious and politically divided. As such, warfare between petty kingdoms accounts for much of Breton history. What it means to be a Breton is considered an age-old quandary by their people. However, knighthood is pivotal to Breton identity. The chivalrous knights of old set an example for future generations by embarking on grand quests in search of adventure and glory. In their free time, Breton youths from all walks of life continue this tradition by engaging in noble pursuits, either in thought or in action.

    <p>
      The {% bemph "Dunmer" %}, also known as Dark Elves, are the grey-skinned, red-eyed elves of Morrowind. &raquo;&hairsp;Dark&hairsp;&laquo; is variously interpreted as meaning &raquo;&hairsp;dark-skinned&hairsp;&laquo;, &raquo;&hairsp;gloomy&hairsp;&laquo;, or &raquo;&hairsp;ill-favored by fate&hairsp;&laquo;, with the Dunmer and their national identity embracing all these various connotations with enthusiasm. Their combination of powerful intellects with a strong and agile physique often produce great warriors and sorcerers. Dunmer distrust and are distrusted by most other cultures. They are often called proud, ruthless, and xenophobic, but greatly value loyalty and family. The history of the Dunmer begins with the Chimer, an Aldmeri splinter group that followed the prophet Veloth to present-day Morrowind, seeking religious freedom. They now predominantly worship the three living gods of the Tribunal: Almalexia, Sotha Sil, and Vivec, also known together as {% daedric "ASV" %}. Dark Elven society is currently divided between the nomadic Ashlander tribes and the settled Great Houses, which represent the dominant culture. Political machinations are constant, and assassinations are common, with the legally established Morag Tong providing such services. Enslavement of Argonians and Khajiit is commonplace, although many men and mer are also enslaved. Despite the claim that owning slaves is their right, many Dunmer still find the practice abhorrent and immoral.

    <p>
      {% bemph "Gnolls" %} are a digitigrade, hyena-like beastfolk scattered across temperate mainland Tamriel. They possess coarse, spotted fur and a spinal mane, a robust skeletal structure, and extremely powerful jaws capable of exerting enough bite force to easily shatter bones. Gnolls originate from the province now called Hammerfell. However, during the Yokudan migrations in the First Era, they were displaced from their homeland, and can now be found all over Tamriel, as far south as Senchal, and as far north as Evermor. Gnolls are known for their keen sense of smell and iron-clad stomach, enabling them to enjoy foods that would be utterly unpalatable to other species. Though they are even better known for their profound laziness and aversion to work. Gnolls tend to value comfort a lot. It is also the only prospect that will motivate them to embark on more ambitious projects, except for coercion, of course. Thus, even the Empire is reluctant to conscript Gnolls, despite their impressive physique. Hence, they occasionally operate as marauders or bounty hunters, but only when their current way of life is under threat or when they need more coin to afford luxuries. Consequently, Gnolls are often marginalised and mistrusted by other cultures, even though they rarely instigate hostilities. All they seek is a comfortable place to stay and a plentiful supply of rich, hearty food.
    </p>

    <p>
      {% bemph "Imperials" %}, also known as Cyrodiils, are the well-educated and cosmopolitan human natives of Tamriel’s central province. Though physically less imposing than the Nords and possessing less innate magical aptitude than the Bretons, Imperials are renowned for their unmatched diplomatic acumen, commercial enterprise, and rigid military discipline. Imperial society is broadly divided into two distinct cultural spheres: the wealthy, magically inclined, and religiously complex Nibenese of the eastern river valleys, and the rugged, fiercely independent, and martial Colovians of the western highlands. Through their extraordinary skills in statecraft and the deployment of their highly trained, well-equipped armies, they have historically managed to subjugate most other nations, establishing two of the continent's most enduring empires. Currently, the Emperor Tiber Septim is attempting to forge the third. Imperials view themselves as the rightful rulers and civilisers of the known world, leveraging their vast trade networks, standardised laws, and the unifying faith of the Eight Divines to maintain order and force assimilation across their sprawling territory. However, the Aldmeri Dominion has no intention of submitting to another upstart, Black Marsh defends its borders through its hostile nature, and no mere army can overcome the Tribunal’s strength.
    </p>

  <div class="text-wrap">
    <p>
      The {% bemph "Khajiit" %}, meaning &raquo;&hairsp;desert-walker&hairsp;&laquo; in their native language, Ta'agra, are cat-like people from Elsweyr, a southern coastal province defined by arid badlands and dense tropical jungles. While they are renowned for their extraordinary intelligence and agility, Khajiit are most unique in their physiological connection to the ja-Kha'jay, the Lunar Lattice. A Khajiit's morphology is entirely determined by the phases of the moons, Jone and Jode, at the time of their birth. This results in wildly diverse morphotypes ranging from the quadrupedal, housecat-sized Alfiq to the towering Pahmar-raht tiger men. Khajiiti society is deeply mystical and heavily centered around the cultivation and consumption of moon sugar, a narcotic regarded as sacred and believed to be made from crystallised moonlight. Consequently, while outsiders frequently dismiss them as habitual smugglers, drug addicts, and common thieves, the Khajiit possess a complex, spiritual culture bound by an ancient reverence for the moons and their own divine pantheon. Roughly 300 years ago, the Elsweyr Confederacy, composed of the two Kingdoms Anequina in the north and Pellitine in the south,  was ravaged by the outbreak of the Knahaten Flu. Unexpected help came from the Altmer, bringing direly needed healers and supplies. Thus, 20 years later, Elsweyr joined the Altmeri Dominion.
    </p>

  <div class="text-wrap">
    <p>
      {% bemph "Nords" %} are the tall, fair-haired humans native to the freezing and mountainous province of Skyrim, in northern Tamriel. They are renowned for their defiant physical endurance and martial prowess. Fierce, proud, and deeply passionate, Nords make for enthusiastic warriors, intrepid scouts, and skilful craftsmen, whose deeds have shaped Tamrielic history. Although other cultures often perceive them as boisterous, superstitious, and xenophobic, Nords also place great importance on personal honour and hospitality. According to legend, their history begins on the continent of Atmora, from which the Nords migrated to Tamriel under the command of Ysgramor. Politically, their society is torn between the cosmopolitan Western Holds, which have historically embraced Cyrodiilic customs, and the more isolationist Old Holds of Eastern Skyrim, which fiercely guard what they perceive as the traditional Nordic ways. They view the arcane arts with deep-seated suspicion, trusting instead in the visceral honesty of steel and clinging to their religious traditions with staunch reverence. While traditionally minded Nords are driven by a foundational, cultural obsession with valour and a glorious warrior’s death in combat, such ambitions are diminishing in significance among younger Nords with more secular perspectives.

    <p>
      The {% bemph "Orcs" %}, also known as the Orsimer, meaning &raquo;&hairsp;Pariah Folk&hairsp;&laquo;, are often mistaken for Betmer, but actually appear to be of elven descent. They are a muscular, green-skinned and tusked people found all over Tamriel, but especially in the Wrothgarian Mountains of northern High Rock. In the past, Orcs were widely feared and hated by the other species of Tamriel, seen as nothing more than uncivilized beasts and savage barbarians. However, through their discipline, outstanding craftsmanship, and martial prowess, they have slowly gained acceptance, especially for their distinguished service in the Empire's legions. Nowadays, Orc warriors in heavy armour are among the finest front-line troops Tiber Septim can muster, and those who dwell in the Empire's cities are as civilised as any of the other citizens, having worked hard to be seen as people in their own right. The exact provenance of the Orsimer is the subject of conflicting historical accounts. According to the predominant Aldmeri myth, they are the cursed descendants of an elven splinter group that worshiped Trinimac. According to this myth, when Boethiah corrupted Trinimac, transforming him into Malacath, the Prince of Ash and Bone, his followers were simultaneously corrupted into the Orsimer.

  <div class="text-wrap">
    <p>
      {% bemph "Redguards" %} are the human phenotype that primarily inhabits the province of Hammerfell. Their lost homeland is the continent of Yokuda, which sank into the sea during the First Era. Thus, the Yokudan survivors fled east to Tamriel, and the Ra’gada warriors conquered the lands of present-day Hammerfell. The Tamrielic name for the foreign invaders, &raquo;&hairsp;Redguard&hairsp;&laquo;, is derived from this term. They are easily recognised by their dark, even ebony complexion, unique amongst men, as well as their tall and finely toned physique, paired with a hardy constitution that allows them to thrive in the hot and dry climate of their new home. Redguards are widely recognised for their love of travelling, adventuring, and sailing the high seas. They can be frequently encountered throughout Tamriel as sailors and mercenaries. Their Yokudan heritage gifted them with advanced knowledge of seafaring, agriculture, military strategy, and even astronomy, allowing them to thrive where others could merely hope to survive. Their society is extremely martial, and nearly everyone is expected to have at least a basic grasp of weaponry and combat, although only the ruling class is generally expected to have detailed knowledge of strategy and tactics.
